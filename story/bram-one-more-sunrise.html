How his life is written

Every blade is a verdict.

Bram returns to your counter five times, climbing one rung of a life with each visit — but only if your steel keeps him alive to climb it.

He lives, and rises Fine · Epic

A good sword carries him to the next station — and he comes back grander, asking for a blade to match. Epic lifts him gloriously; Fine lets him survive, scarred.

He dies, and the book shuts Weak

A failed blade kills him where he stands. Someone who loved him walks in once — not to buy, but to tell you how it ended.

Two threads run the whole climb: ☀ Sunrises — all he thinks he's buying is one more. ⚖ The debt — he can't pay at first, and always, always pays what he owes.

I

Rung I · Day 1

The Desperate Man

🛡️ Durable

He asks — "I'm not a fighting man, I'll not pretend it. I just need — anything. Something that won't shatter first swing. I've a woman and a boy behind me and I only need to hold the dark off till sunrise. One more sunrise. I can't pay today. But I pay what I owe."

If the steel holds → he lives

He holds the ravine mouth long enough for Nessa and the boy Tomkin to get clear. A retreating muster sees his face — and takes him.

"It held. Gods, it HELD — I hit a thing twice my size and the blade laughed at it. I felt like somebody. I've not felt like somebody in a long while."

If the steel fails → he dies

Closure · Nessa, the widow

"He held the ravine with the broken half till we were over the ridge. This whetstone was his father's — all he had that wasn't borrowed. He said you were an honest hand. It cost him everything to be right about you."
II

Rung II · Day 2

The Raw Recruit

⚖️ Balanced

He returns — washed, fed, taller somehow — "Look at me, eh? They took me. Actual muster, actual pay. The woman and the boy have a roof now, a real one. But that scrap's no soldier's blade, and I'm a soldier now. Give me something honest and even. Balanced. Something that does what I tell it — since half the time I've no idea what I'm doing."

If the steel holds → he rises

He survives his first true muster and doesn't run. Lads stand alive beside him because of it, and a sergeant marks him.

"I didn't run. That's the whole miracle, I didn't run — and there were lads alive next to me on account of it. On account of you. I'm keeping a tally, smith. What I owe. It's getting long."

If the steel fails → he dies

Closure · Odgar, his file-mate

"Recruit Bram. My file. Blade folded at the muster and he — they say it was quick. He talked about you. Said the smith at the Ember was the only clean thing left in the Pass. The widow couldn't stand in here again. So they sent me."
III

Rung III · Day 3

The Named Fighter

⚔️ Sharp

There's a swagger in him now — but he still sets his coin down first — "They've a name for me down the line. I'll not repeat it, it'll go to my head. But a man with a name needs an edge worth the name — proper Sharp, the kind that parts a thing before it knows it's parted. You gave me the arm, back when I was a beggar bleeding on your floor. I remember it every single day."

If the steel holds → he climbs

He becomes the man they put where the line's about to break — a fixture of the front, and officers begin to watch him.

"It's an extension of the thinking, this one. I don't swing it, I just decide, and it's already done. That's not a sword, smith — that's a conversation. Best one I have all week."

If the steel fails → he dies

Closure · Tomkin, the widow's boy

"They call'd him the Sharp. Like there was only one. His sword broke — he's the best there ever was and his SWORD broke. Mam said you'd want to know. She said you were family, near enough, since you're the one kept making him. He'd want you paid."
IV

Rung IV · Day 4

The Captain

🌩️ Storm

He fills the doorway now, a captain's sash, grey at the temples — "They gave me a company. Me — with lads looking at me the way I once looked at men who could actually fight. I don't need an edge for one man anymore. I need a blade a hundred can SEE, up at the front in the worst of it, so they follow it into the storm instead of breaking. Give me a Storm of my own."

If the steel holds → he leads

His company holds where others rout. His name becomes the thing that steadies the whole Pass — and, at his best, moves the line forward.

"We took GROUND. Off the Gray — nobody takes ground off the Gray. Stood at dusk on dirt that was theirs at dawn, every man of mine alive on it. I keep thinking of the ravine. You've sold me a thousand sunrises now."

If the steel fails → he dies

Closure · Sergeant Halla, his second

"Captain went down where a captain's meant to be. Blade failed him — and he still got the company clear on foot, roaring at us to run while he didn't. The boy Tomkin's outside. He won't come in. Says the door's too full of him."
V

Rung V · Day 5 · The Reckoning

The Legend

☄️ Celestial

He comes at dawn, alone, quiet as a held breath — "Last one, smith. They want me to break the Gray's own line at Coldwater, and a thing like that needs a blade the songs can carry — Celestial, if you've ever had it in you. You armed a beggar who couldn't hold a hoe. Look what you built. So build the end of it too. It's not enough coin. It was never going to be enough. But I pay what I owe — you taught me that."

If it's Epic — the legend

The Dawnward

Mostly Epic all the way up

He comes back down the road at sunrise and the Gray does not come with him. He hangs your first cheap Durable blade — the beggar's blade — on your forge wall himself. He grows old. Tomkin names his own boy Bram. When you finally close the forge, the whole Pass turns out — everyone in it alive on a sunrise you once sold a dead man on credit.

If it's Fine — the cost

The Scarred Victory

Survived, but on lesser steel

He breaks the line — and it breaks him doing it. The Gray recedes, doesn't die. He comes home grey and quiet, sits by Nessa's fire and flinches at dusk. He still visits, still pays what he owes. "We did it," he says, and means it, and never quite smiles again. It was enough. It had to be.

Rung V · If the last blade fails

He dies at the threshold of winning.

No one person can carry this closure, so the Pass sends a girl who's been paid to learn his song.

Closure · a fiddler in the square

"I'm to sing it tonight, and they told me the last verse happens here, so I've come to get it right. They say he broke the Gray's line and it cost him the last blow — sword gave at the very end, and he finished it with his hands. Won the war and didn't live an hour past it."

She lifts the fiddle, then lowers it. "The song calls you 'the honest hand that made him.' Is that a good verse, or an unkind one? I couldn't tell, writing it."

The whole life, in one law

You are the hand that fills.

Epic

He climbs, gloriously. Push for these and he ends a shining legend — the Dawnward.

Fine

He climbs, scarred. All-Fine still wins the war, but he ends a tired, quiet man.

Weak

He dies. One closure scene plays, and his book shuts for good.
