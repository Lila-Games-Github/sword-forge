Potion Craft: Alchemist Simulator

A visual teardown of the game Sword Forge is modelled on — with emphasis on its signature spatial-crafting mechanic and the design lessons it holds for a grid-based blacksmith game.

🏗️ niceplay games 📦 Publisher tinyBuild 📅 Full release 13 Dec 2022 🖥️ PC · Console · Mobile 🎯 Metacritic 74

The one-sentence thesis

Everything in Potion Craft hangs off a single idea, executed with unusual polish.

You don't pick a recipe from a list — you steer a potion marker across a fogged 2D map, plotting paths with ingredients and locking in effects by aligning the marker over target zones. The whole game lives or dies on how good that navigation feels.
💡 The developer said it outright. Niceplay CEO Mikhail Chuprakov (DevGAMM 2024): “If I couldn't make the control perfect, the game would never work.” — Gamereactor

03

⭐ The Alchemy Map — the signature mechanic

This is why Potion Craft is the reference. Crafting is a spatial puzzle: navigate a marker toward effect zones by plotting and manipulating a path. Below: the anatomy of one brew.

The six tool verbs

ActionWhat it does to the map
⚗️GrindSets how far the marker travels along the ingredient's vector. More grinding = farther.
🌿Add ingredientPlots a path in a fixed direction, appended from the marker's current spot.
🥄StirIrreversibly advances the marker forward along the plotted path.
💧Ladle / basePulls the marker straight back to origin (not a retrace). Dilution.
🔥BellowsLocks in an effect while the marker overlaps an effect zone.
🧂SaltsRotate the path (Sun ↻ / Moon ↺), erase it (Void), or pull toward an effect (Philosopher's).

Why it's satisfying

  • Multiple routes reach the same effect — designed deliberately for player expression.
  • Discovery = exploration. Fog hides effect zones (shown as “?”); new ingredients reveal new regions. No recipe list to memorise.
  • Failure is cheap. Almost no punishment for a “wrong” brew — experimentation is the point.
  • Two-axis navigation. Position and rotation both matter for alignment.
  • Bases swap the whole board — water, oil (adds slowing “swamp” terrain), and wine each have their own map.

Alignment precision → effect tier

Pump the bellows while the marker overlaps an effect zone. The closer the overlap, the higher the tier (I → III), filling more of the potion's 5 effect slots.

Sources: Fandom — Alchemy Map, Ladle, Grokipedia. Verified 3-0.

04

Economy & customers

The “sell” half of the loop is a moral choice engine, not a vending machine.

Requests

■ Green = optional. Meeting it makes the customer pay more (a price multiplier).
■ Red = mandatory. Fail it and the customer walks — no sale.

Requests are often vague and may add up to ~2 optional + ~2 mandatory conditions: ingredients to include/exclude, base/salt limits, ingredient counts, or required qualities.

Haggling & buying

Ingredients are bought from travelling merchants — successful haggling saves coin — or grown in a garden. Reviewers repeatedly call the haggling shallow.

Reputation is a two-sided moral scale

Bounded −100 → +100. You gain “good” standing both by helping honest customers and by refusing malicious requests. Your position changes who walks in the door.

Sources: Fandom — Requests, Reputation, TheGamer. Verified 3-0. Note: “popularity” is a separate track from reputation.

Gold funds a tiered Alchemy Machine (20,000g total) that gates salts and a chain of legendary recipes.

12,000g
  • Full unlock
  • Unlocks Rubedo & the Philosopher's Stone
Nigredo→ Albedo→ Citrinitas→ Rubedo→ ✦ Philosopher's Stone

Each legendary recipe is discovered by crafting the previous one. Recipe/effect discovery otherwise happens by exploring the fogged map. Sources: Fandom — Alchemy Machine, Attack of the Fanboy. Costs 3-0; recipe order 2-1 (corroborated).

⚠️ No separate “talent tree” was found. Progression = machine tiers + recipe discovery. If Sword Forge wants a skill tree, Potion Craft is not precedent for one.
06

Game feel & art direction

📜

Alchemy-manual look

Hand-drawn, parchment-and-ink aesthetic. The UI is frequently praised as lovingly crafted — the map literally reads as an illustrated notebook.

✋

Tactile inputs

Every action is a physical verb — grind, stir, pump — not a menu click. Manipulation feel is the design pillar.

🌙

Cozy, low-stress

Unhurried, ambient. “No punishment for failure” is a tone as much as a mechanic.

✅ Borrow

  1. Obsess over control feel of the grid. The dev says the game only worked because manipulation felt perfect. This is lesson #1 — the tap/drag/hammer feel must be the highest-polish thing in the build.
  2. Multiple routes to the same result. Reachability-by-different-paths is what made experimentation fun and gave replay value.
  3. “Failure is a feature.” Cheap, low-punishment experimentation lowers the floor while keeping a high ceiling.
  4. Green/red order conditions — an elegant, self-teaching risk/reward + price-multiplier system.
  5. Precision → quality tier as the core reward mapping.

⛔ Avoid (learn from its criticism)

  1. The “one clever mechanic wears thin” trap. Potion Craft's single biggest critique. A novel grid mechanic is not a full game — budget real design time for the back half: variety, escalating goals, a non-grindy endgame.
  2. Endgame that collapses into grinding. Keep new verbs / tiles / modifiers coming, not just bigger gold numbers.
  3. A shallow economy. “Weak haggling” was a recurring ding — give selling real decisions.
  4. Assuming the mobile economy is solved. Potion Craft is premium PC/console; its mobile monetization is unproven. Sword Forge's is its own problem.
